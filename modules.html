Learning To Learn - Cubing

Learning to learn, one of the first modules I completed at the beginning of this course, had my classmates and I dive into something I had never learned, experimented with, or even done in my life, cubing. By watching the documentary our class was provided, I got to learn about cubing and the impacts that it had on many lives across the world, and the relationships and experiences that the people in the documentary were able to develop based on cubing as well. At the end of the documentary, my group was instructed to make a slideshow talking about a specific topic and how it was addressed or covered in the film, and my group chose the topic of friendship. I have been in many scenarios before in which I’ve had to try new things and make new connections, and cubing helped me do them all over again, just like the people in the documentary. I made some new friends through this module and through cubing, as I got two of my classmates to teach me how to solve a Pyraminx. Ever since then I’ve been improving my times, working to understand the best way to solve the Pyraminx and understand the algorithms that come with it too. Before I had started all of this, I had no idea how intense cubing could be, and how fun it would become for me. Learning and practicing the solving of a Pyraminx eventually led me to my best solve, at 17.17 seconds, a time I am proud of, speaking of my previous times from the beginning of my cubing journey, which started at around 3 minutes and 30 seconds. Not only did this module introduce me to something new through cubing, I got to understand how to solve a Pyraminx and what cubing meant to others, which helped me find new friends as well along the way.
